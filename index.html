Forge with PID Temperature Control

Forge concept I proposed, designed, and built for the MIT Blacksmithing Lab. I performed a majority of the fabrication, welding, and assembly of components. Uses a thermocouple inside the forge itself for feedback, and restricts gas flow to manipulate temperature.

Testing the forge.

Solar Conservation Drone

Developed in a collaboration between MIT Photovoltaics Lab and Conservation International. I led a student team in desigining and building a long-range quadcopter with an integrated solar panel allowing it to autonomously recharge over long-distance data collecting missions.

Automating Seaweed Farming

Seaweed farming is an industry dominated by manual labor. As a continuation of my senior thesis, which focused on evaluating methods of automation, I built a prototype machine to reduce the labor involved in farming seaweed. This project is ongoing.

A previous iteration of design, testing, and evaluation.

"Mass-Production" Yoyo

Designed a NASCAR-inspired yoyo with thermoformed and injection-molded parts, and manufactured 50 over the course of 48 hours. Design involved precise CNC machining for snap fits and accounting for tolerances and shrinkages involved in the various fabrication processes.

In addition to contributing to the overall design, I was solely responsible for the production of the hubcap pictured above. This involved using a 3-axis mill for the mold, thermal simulation of the injection molding process, release mechanism design, and other design considerations to allow part separation and filling.
